SEO-Optimised Listings That Rank on Marketplaces & Google

A perfectly optimised product listing ranks inside the marketplace algorithm AND on Google SERP simultaneously — capturing organic buyers from both channels with zero additional ad spend. We engineer every element of your listing for maximum discoverability and conversion.

🔬 Product Listing Anatomy — BDM Optimisation SEO-Powered
1
Product Title HIGH IMPACT
The single most important ranking factor. We research high-volume keywords and craft titles following each platform's algorithm preferences — Brand + Primary Keyword + Key Features + Size/Colour.
e.g. "Sony WH-1000XM5 Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones, 30Hr Battery, Bluetooth 5.2, Black"
2
Bullet Points / Key Features HIGH IMPACT
5 keyword-rich bullets that highlight benefits over features — indexed by Amazon/Flipkart algorithm. We map secondary keywords strategically across all 5 bullets.
e.g. "Industry-leading noise cancellation | 30-hour battery | Multipoint connection to 2 devices"
3
Product Description & A+ Content HIGH IMPACT
2,000-character keyword-rich description for organic ranking + A+ Enhanced Content (brand registered sellers) with comparison charts, lifestyle images and expanded brand storytelling.
A+ Content increases conversion by 5–20% on Amazon
4
Backend Search Terms MEDIUM IMPACT
Hidden keyword fields (Amazon: 250 bytes) for synonyms, misspellings and complementary search terms — never visible to customers but indexed by the algorithm.
e.g. "wireless headphone bluetooth earphone over ear noise block premium audio"
5
Images (Main + Lifestyle + Infographic) HIGH IMPACT
White-background main image + 6–8 lifestyle and infographic images with keyword-rich ALT text. Images drive CTR — which is a direct ranking signal on all major platforms.
7 images minimum: Main, angles, in-use, size chart, feature callout, comparison, packaging
6
Pricing, Reviews & Ratings HIGH IMPACT
Competitive pricing analysis, review velocity strategies, ratings management and Q&A optimisation — all critical ranking signals weighted by the marketplace algorithm.
4.5+ stars = 89% more downloads vs 4.0 star equivalent products

FAQ

eCommerce Marketing Questions Answered

Which marketplace should I prioritise first?

+

It depends on your product category, target market and brand positioning. For India, start with Amazon.in or Flipkart based on your category. For USA, Amazon is essential. For handmade/unique products, Etsy is unmatched. For Southeast Asia, Shopee leads. We assess your product, margins, competition and category before recommending the right platform sequence — ensuring you build momentum before expanding to additional marketplaces.

What is ACOS and how do you optimise it?

+

ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sale) is the percentage of ad-attributed revenue spent on advertising. A 20% ACOS means ₹20 spent for every ₹100 in sales. The ideal ACOS varies by category and margin — we calculate your break-even ACOS (typically 15–35%) and manage campaigns to achieve profitability. For new products, we accept higher ACOS initially to build ranking, then reduce it as organic ranking improves and lowers our total advertising dependency.

Can my product listing rank on Google SERP as well as Amazon?

+

Yes — and this dual ranking is one of the most powerful (and underutilised) strategies in eCommerce. Amazon, Flipkart and major marketplace product pages are indexed by Google. When your listing is optimised with the right keywords, it can appear in Google organic results, Google Shopping ads AND the marketplace search simultaneously. We specifically optimise titles and descriptions to satisfy both algorithms, generating free traffic from Google in addition to your marketplace organic ranking.

What is A+ Content and do I need it?

+

A+ Content (Enhanced Brand Content on Amazon) is an enhanced product description available to Brand Registered sellers — replacing the standard text description with rich modules including images, comparison charts, feature highlights and brand storytelling. Amazon reports A+ Content increases conversion rates by 5–20% and reduces return rates. It's not required, but for any brand-registered seller doing meaningful volume, it delivers a clear and measurable ROI on the investment.

How do you handle multiple marketplace accounts?

+

We use a dedicated marketplace management system to manage all your accounts from a single dashboard — tracking inventory levels, order velocity, listing health scores, ad performance and customer ratings across every platform simultaneously. Each platform has its own dedicated campaign structure, optimised listings and performance benchmarks, but we use unified data to make cross-platform budget and strategy decisions that maximise total GMV.

How do I expand my brand to international marketplaces?

+

International eCommerce expansion involves: choosing the right market (based on your product demand, competition and margins), setting up seller accounts in that market (Amazon USA, eBay UK, Shopee Singapore etc.), localising listings (language, currency, local keywords), handling cross-border logistics and returns, and managing currency and tax compliance. We guide you through every step — from market selection to first sale — and manage your international marketplace presence ongoing.

What is the difference between FBA and FBF?

+

FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) means you ship your inventory to Amazon's warehouse and Amazon handles storage, picking, packing, shipping and returns. FBF (Fulfillment by Flipkart) is the equivalent on Flipkart. Both programmes boost your Prime/Plus eligibility and typically result in better search ranking. We advise on which fulfilment model makes economic sense for your products based on dimensions, margins and sales velocity — and help you set up FBA/FBF logistics when required.

Do you manage Shopify stores as well as marketplaces?

+

Yes — Shopify D2C store management is part of our eCommerce offering. We set up, optimise and market your Shopify store alongside your marketplace presence, driving direct traffic through Google Shopping, Meta Ads, email marketing and SEO while using marketplace data to inform your D2C strategy. Many brands run both simultaneously — using marketplaces for discovery and their Shopify store for higher-margin repeat purchases.
