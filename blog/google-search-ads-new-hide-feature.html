Google Search Ads New Hide Feature: What You Need to Know

Google has introduced a new feature allowing users to hide sponsored search results, making it easier to navigate the search page. This guide explains what’s changing, how it works, and why it matters to both users and advertisers in 2025.

1. What’s New with Google Ads?

Google has introduced a new feature that groups all ads into one section under the **“Sponsored Results”** label, making it easier for users to distinguish between organic and paid search results. You’ll also find a **“Hide Sponsored Results”** button, allowing you to collapse the sponsored ads after viewing them.

2. Why This Matters for Users and Advertisers

This change allows users to navigate Google Search results with greater ease, reducing clutter at the top of the page. For advertisers, it means the ad format will be more transparent, which may impact click-through rates and engagement.

3. How to Hide Ads in Google Search

Once you’ve viewed the sponsored results, a button will appear allowing you to hide them. This option is manual, but it gives users greater control over their search experience.

4. The Impact of This Change on Ads Performance

For advertisers, the new layout could change how users engage with search results. Ads that are visually distinct might see fewer clicks, especially from users who are more selective about which content they engage with.

5. New Ad Labels and Transparency

The **“Sponsored”** label will now be used consistently across all paid ad formats, including Shopping results. This provides clearer transparency, helping users easily identify which results are paid placements.
